We talk a lot about "AI Agents" that can do things for us, not just talk to us. But in the world of **commerce and retail**, agents have hit a wall: fragmentation. Every store has a different checkout, different API, and different logic. How agents can be new users of the existing eCommerce and retail platforms?

Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) [**https://ucp.dev/**](https://ucp.dev/) could be a massive step forward in this direction.

The Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) is an open standard designed specifically to give AI agents a common language to discover, buy, and manage products across different platforms without needing custom, one-off integrations for every single merchant.

**Why this is a game-changer for AI Agentic Interfaces in Shopping:**

**Standardized "Agentic" Commerce:** UCP provides the building blocks (Checkout, Identity Linking, Order Management) that allow an AI agent to actually complete a purchase journey—from discovery to final sale—seamlessly.

**Interoperability by Design:** Instead of building custom connections for every retailer, UCP creates a unified standard. It supports critical frameworks like **Agent Payments Protocol (AP2)** and **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**, ensuring different AI systems and shopping platforms can finally talk to each other.

**Retailer-Centric Control:** Crucially for the industry, it keeps the business at the center. Retailers remain the "Merchant of Record," retaining ownership of the customer relationship and business rules, even when an AI agent handles the transaction.

**Who is backing this?** This isn't just a theory; it is co-developed and adopted by industry giants including **Google, Shopify, Walmart, Target, Etsy, and Wayfair**.

UCP is the infrastructure we've been waiting for in order to move from general-purpose AI chat-bots to something really useful for consumers mediated by AI agents and standardized protocols like UCP. What would be next industry to do the same?
