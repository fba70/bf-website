Question: is digital product content management done right in large enterprises?

Let's try to answer it in this article.

One could probably notice a trend that digital product content management (or product information management) tools have grown significantly within last 10 years to support different internet and E-commerce scenarios which are driven by the following factors:

Relatively simple product modelsDynamic product contentComplicated integration environment between distributed platforms over internetHuge demand due to the general growth on internet services and platforms.

Still for large enterprises (like telecommunication operators, banks, insurance companies, industrial vendors, etc.) it was not the case. Their product management landscape in normally driven by the following factors:

Product models are complicatedBusiness and technological verticals are occupied by well-known vendors for related domains (like BSS, OSS, CRM, ERP, CMS & digital front-end, etc. vendors)Each vendor offers their own vision of product management modelling and toolsImplementation of centralised product management platforms is complicated and can be hardly motivated from business case perspective as those platforms are quite expensive.

That results in inefficient product lifecycle management processes which require a lot of manual work of content managers in multiple systems. High costs, low quality of product content, high overhead for product and operational departments.
